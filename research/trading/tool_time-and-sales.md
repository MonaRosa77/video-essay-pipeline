# Time & Sales (Tape Reading) — Learning Resources

**What it is**: The raw stream of executed trades showing price, size, time, and whether each trade hit the bid or lifted the ask. Confirms whether trades are aggressive (market orders hitting bid/ask) or passive. Used to read speed/urgency, detect institutional size, and validate what you see on the price ladder.

**Used in**: Trend Scalp (reading urgency/FOMO via tape speed), Exhaustion Flick (confirming forced buying), Continuation Scalp (reading pace changes during pullbacks), all strategies (confirming aggression direction)

## Key Concepts to Learn

| Concept | What to Look For |
|---------|-----------------|
| **Aggression** | Are market orders hitting the bid (sellers aggressive) or lifting the ask (buyers aggressive)? |
| **Speed/pace** | How fast are trades printing? Acceleration = urgency. Deceleration = momentum fading. |
| **Size** | Are large orders appearing? Clustered at one price = institutional interest. |
| **Icebergs** | More volume trading at a level than was visible on the book = hidden orders. |
| **Absorption** | Large volume transacting but price not moving = passive participant absorbing aggression. |
| **Spoofing** | Large orders placed and pulled before execution = fake liquidity to influence direction. |
| **Personality shift** | Change in the character of the tape — urgency subsiding, bid backing off, pace changing. |
