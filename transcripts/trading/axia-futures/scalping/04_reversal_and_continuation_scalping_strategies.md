## Core Philosophy

> "Either the moves are doing what we expect or we are out."

Near-zero tolerance for deviation from expected market flow. Order-flow analysis serves as the primary tool. Patience is crucial in trading, but traders must understand **when** to be patient and when not to be.

---

## Strategy 1: Oil Inventory Number (Continuation Scalp)

### Context
Inventory build release comes in **below expectations**, causing a negative market reaction. Rather than trading the initial number release, the strategy **waits for momentum direction confirmation** and pauses to identify reversal entry points.

### Entry Setup -- 4 Key Clues

1. **Number is released; market drops rapidly**
2. **Seek primary momentum (short direction) followed by pullback**
3. **During pullback, watch for struggle to move higher** -- slowdown and inability to advance
4. **Access point identified around price levels 71-72**

### Position Management

- **Stop Loss:** Tight, just above **71-72** (a couple of ticks)
- **Target:** Next pause point on the tape

### Exit Strategy

> "Don't aim for the new low, don't aim for large swings. Aim for a point where it does exactly the same as with the pullback -- the slow down in price action."

- **Profit target area:** **45-50 zone** identified as exit area
- Focus on **order-flow activity changes** rather than large swings
- Watch the price ladder activity for slowdowns that indicate target zones

## Strategy 2: DAX Reversal Scalp

### Setup Recognition
Tail formation at the low, followed by tight range formation held above that tail.

### Step-by-Step Execution

1. **Initial move:** Price moves lower, establishing a tail candle
2. **Range formation:** Tight range formed and maintained above the tail (red rectangle on chart)
3. **Failed breakout:** Initiative breaks out of the range to continue downside but **fails**
4. **Entry signal:** Move below level **70** and back above = access signal to go long
5. **Position entry:** Jump above **70** triggers entry
6. **Stop loss:** Tight stop at level **65**
7. **Trade characteristic:** "Something that you won't be able to see without the order-flow"

### Critical Note
Price ladder action is essential for identifying the exact moment of the failed breakout around the **70** level.

### Target Strategy
Same as Oil example: "Aim for the very short-term level-to-level move or slowdown in price action."

## Key Principle: What You're Playing For

You are playing for **order-flow activity change** -- the moment where the price ladder behavior shifts. This is not about predicting levels; it's about reading the real-time change in participant behavior.
