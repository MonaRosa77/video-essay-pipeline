## Core Focus

The traders' capacity to interpret the **inverse correlation between fixed-income products** -- specifically the German Bund and the Italian BTP.

> "Based on this correlation, widening spread between German Bund and Italian BTP, this trader was able to place large bets in Bund and hold onto buying as long as BTP has offered."

---

## Market Context

The trader exploited market reaction to **"the announcement of Euro-skeptic Paolo Savona being a possible candidate for the economic minister"** during the Italian government crisis.

---

## First Leg Analysis

**Theme:** Inverse correlation between Italian 10-Year BTP and the German Bund during the Italian government crisis.

### Study Questions & Answers

1. **What happened to the correlation throughout the week?**
   → It was **diminishing** (correlations breaking apart as news got discounted)

2. **How did this trader use the correlation in his favor?**
   → **BTP going short, for Bund going long.** He leaned on BTP selling to justify Bund buying.

3. **Did the BTP-Bund correlation move best in Risk On or Risk Off?**
   → **Risk On environment**

**Video timestamp:** Trading session starts at **4:22**

## Second Leg Analysis

### Study Questions & Answers

4. **How does this trader scale into trades?**
   → Either by **hitting market** or **resting bids directly below current price**

5. **What is the signal for the end of the move in Bund?**
   → **Fast 5 ticks jump in the BTP.** This signaled the end of the move in Bund.

## Key Trading Clues (Summary)

1. Understand what market theme is in play
2. Understand the correlations between markets when these themes are in play
3. Identify what type of strategy is in play: BTP-BUND spread widening
4. Notice the **diminishing value of news throughout the week** -- correlations breaking apart as the news are getting discounted
5. What moves best in Risk-On and Risk-Off environments
