## Core Concept

> "One of the key qualities of some of the most successful traders is their ability to interpret and analyse new information at lightning speed, so that they can take a position in the shortest possible time."

---

## Date: Thursday, 23rd April 2020

### Opening Loss

The day began poorly: trader was **"caught short on a Gilt (UK 10-year Bond) position early in the morning and took a decent loss."**

Despite the setback, he "managed to stay focused and turn around the day to build a very solid upday" through executing "big positions on several news comments throughout the day."

---

## Trade 1: Bank of Japan -- Unlimited Bond Buying

**News:** BOJ sources indicated readiness to conduct **"unlimited bond buying in order to boost the economy after the Coronavirus fallout."**

**Market impact:** Put pressure on the Yen.

**Action:** Positioned to profit from Yen weakness resulting from BOJ's expansionary policy.

---

## Trade 2: Merkel Comments on EU Response

**News:** Merkel urged **"EU leaders to take a huge response to tackle the pandemic."**

**Interpretation:** Germany showing willingness to a big monetary response = positive for markets.

**Execution:**
- **Sold:** Bund (safe haven bonds)
- **Bought:** EuroStoxx (equities)

**Rationale:** Captured the **"risk-on move"** by going long equities while reducing safe-haven bond exposure.

---

## Trade 3: FT Remdesivir Article -- Initial Reaction

**News:** Financial Times headline: **"Gilead's Antiviral drug Remdesivir had flopped on Clinical trials."**

**Context:** "The market had big hopes that this therapeutic drug was going to slow the spread of the virus."

**Response:** Sold the S&P 500 to capitalize on the knee-jerk sell-off.

---

## Trade 4: Article Content Contradiction -- Cut & Reverse

**Discovery:** Within the FT article, **"Gilead had commented that the results of the trials were inconclusive and thus not accurate."**

The article contained **"contradictory headlines"** between headline and body.

**Action:** Cut short S&P position and **reversed to long S&P** based on the discrepancy.

**Lesson:** Read beyond the headline to the actual article content. Act on the discrepancy faster than the broader market.

---

## Trade 5: EU Summit Failure -- Risk-Off (The Big Trade)

**News:** "About half an hour later, news from the EU summit started hitting the wires, indicating that EU leaders failed to reach an agreement on a stimulus package."

### Position -- Large Risk-Off Trade Across 4 Markets

- **Sold:** EURUSD
- **Sold:** EuroStoxx
- **Sold:** S&P 500 (E-mini)
- **Bought:** Bunds

**Total position size:** **1,000 lots** across 4 markets

**Result:** "Although the market was a bit slow to react, eventually the risk-off move happened" and the trader "managed to finish his day with a very solid P&L."

---

## Key Principles Demonstrated

1. **Speed of information processing:** Interpret "new information at lightning speed" to position before broader market recognition
2. **Multi-market correlation:** Bonds move inversely to equity risk sentiment; currencies reflect regional economic sentiment; equities respond to risk appetite
3. **Headline vs. content analysis:** Distinguish between sensational headlines and substantive content to identify false market reactions
4. **Resilience:** Recovered from initial morning loss through disciplined execution on subsequent opportunities
5. **Scale of execution:** 1,000 lots across 4 correlated markets on a single news event
