## Core Concept: What Is an Exhaustion Move?

Exhaustion moves are **"short-term extensive runs that have a one-time-framing character."** The end of these moves presents a short-term scalping opportunity identified through price ladder and order-flow reading skills.

> "The idea behind profit-taking in the exhaustion move is its nature to mean revert. Simply, the move has traveled 'too far' and participants have lost the energy to push the move forward."

---

## Five Critical Rules

1. **Short-term scalp** -- you are going for a quick profit, not a big distance target
2. **One clip type of trade** -- this is not your full-size type of trade, you go with your **Tier 1 size**
3. **Try once and move away** -- if the move keeps on going higher (or lower), let it go. Don't try the trade over and over
4. **Fading highs/lows** -- you are essentially fading the high or low of the day
5. **Pattern dependency** -- you only trade when the order-flow pattern appears

## Method 1: The Flick (British Pound Futures)

### Setup Characteristics
- One-directional move
- One-time-framing (sequence of higher lows for up moves, or lower highs for down moves)
- Increased volatility

### The Order-Flow Pattern

The pattern starts with **forced buying** (not chosen buying -- this is stop-running / short squeeze territory). The key signal:

> "The price drops quicker down and can't lift the offer anymore. That struggle to lift the offer to take the last high is our order-flow pattern."

### Price Ladder Signals (Step by Step)
1. Extended, vertical upward move with a sequence of higher lows
2. Watch for the **"last flick up"** -- a final push where "people are forced to buy, not choose to buy"
3. First red candle signals a potential top
4. Sudden price drop
5. Market attempts to rally but **cannot lift the offer** to reach the previous high

### Entry
"That struggle to lift the offer to take the last high is our order-flow pattern and our moment to initiate the scalp short."

### Size & Target
- **Size:** Tier 1 (smallest/reduced)
- **Target:** Quick mean-reversion profit (scalp)

## Method 2: The Absorption (Bund Futures)

### Market Condition
"An over-extended move" where "the market stretched itself without moving value."

### The Order-Flow Pattern
Rather than an excess (flick up), this pattern shows **absorption**:

> "Every time we are trying to lift the offer, someone is happy to sit there and absorb."

Multiple rejections -- bids get absorbed at the high rather than lifting higher.

### Entry
When the absorption becomes persistent and the market can no longer make new highs.

### Size & Target
- **Size:** Tier 1
- **Target:** Deeper than The Flick -- this trade can be "held for a bit longer. The idea is still the same, reversion to the mean."

## Risk Management Warning

> "When do you scalp and when you are holding short-term swing? Use the clues we have discussed and never fight the trend. If you are wrong once, there is a chance you will get it wrong a few more times before the market turns. Don't fight it, move on to another trading idea."

---

## Key Distinction Between the Two Methods

| | The Flick | The Absorption |
|---|---|---|
| **Signal** | Failed offer lift after flick up | Persistent absorption at extreme |
| **Nature** | Forced buying exhausts | Passive seller absorbs all buying |
| **Hold time** | Quick scalp | Can hold longer |
| **Target depth** | Shallow | Deeper mean reversion |
| **Instrument** | British Pound | Bund |
