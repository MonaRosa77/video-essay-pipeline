## Core Challenge

> "Scalping consistently well in such a dynamic and fast pace moving market as S&P500 requires true mastery."

Understanding **when** is the right time for the scalp and **what** to look for is crucial.

---

## Entry Formula

**order-flow dynamics (pressure > pace / panic / FOMO) + location**

### Choosing The Right Location

Target areas of **least resistance possible:**
- New all-time highs
- Gaps following accumulation
- Untested levels

---

## The "Personality Shift" Concept

### What You're Looking For (Entry)

The desired condition on the price ladder:
- **"Sticky bid"** or **"relentless bid"** that doesn't retreat
- Sellers uninterested in hitting bids hard
- Bid reloading continuously at each level
- Everybody is trying to get in -- desperation to not miss out
- Wait passively **one or two prices below** while others panic-buy above you

### Exit Signal

> "Personality shift (change of character of the move)"

The bid starts **backing off more rapidly** -- the urgency/FOMO wave is subsiding. When this personality changes, exit.

---

## Four Operational Principles

1. **Get in as others rush to buy** -- desperation to not miss out
2. **Take advantage of urgency to be involved** -- ride the FOMO wave
3. **Get out when move settles** -- be humble with what you are doing
4. **Don't get caught holding for more than is available** -- know when it's over

---

## Trade Selection

- Targets **breaks into all-time highs (ATHs)**
- Requires a high degree of practice and screen time
- **"Don't try to scalp every break, be picky and selective, one trade at a time"**

## Key Takeaways

This strategy is about reading **urgency** and **participant desperation** on the price ladder. You're not predicting where price will go -- you're identifying the moment when everyone is scrambling to get in, riding that wave, and exiting when the scramble subsides.

The "sticky bid" that keeps reloading = your entry confirmation.
The "bid backing off" = your exit signal.
