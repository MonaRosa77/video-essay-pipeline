## Tip 1: High Conviction Setups

> "If you look at professional traders in Axia Futures, many of the 8-figure traders don't have a winning percentage greater than 50/50."

Profitability comes from **"maximizing their high-probability trades"** and **"pushing their size when the odds are highly stacked in their favor."**

### The Danger

New traders assume after 6-12 months they understand high-conviction setups.

> "Maximizing your size in the under-developed setup will lead to greater losses"

...and damages both confidence and the trading account.

### The Lesson

> "You win in trading by maximizing size in your high-conviction setups."

### The Caution

> "Don't forget that sizing up too early in your career might lead to outsized losses and damages your confidence."

**Practical guidance:** Build detailed depth of your best trades before scaling. When you grasp a high-probability setup:

> "Increase size for this particular setup by 2-3x and test it."

## Tip 2: Pattern Is Not A Trade Setup

### The Mistake
New traders focus solely on patterns (flags, triangles) without broader context.

> "Trading only patterns hugely limits your ability to maximize your profit."

### Professional Approach

Axia traders **"build a sequence of clues and triggers so that when the simple flag break comes, for example, they are aiming for a much larger directional move because their understanding of the wider structure gives them greater ability to maximize their profit."**

### The Lesson

> "Don't trade just patterns, understand when the pattern has the highest probability of success."

> "If there is a nice scalp, but opportunity can unlock swing move, don't limit your trade to the scalp."

## Tip 3: Law of Diminishing Marginal Returns

### The Plateau

Early career: "leap jumps in market understanding and frequent dopamine spikes." As knowledge accumulates, "our learning curve starts to stall."

This is natural: **diminishing marginal returns.** The snowball effect comes with more distanced milestones.

### The Recommendation

> "Be patient and focus on skill acquisition."

### Judging Progress

Don't judge by P&L. Instead, measure:

a) **"Eliminating inconsistencies"**
b) **"Sticking to a process that is helping your trading"**

### Tracking System

- Use **weekly and monthly score card system**
- **"Track your trading like it would be a business"**
- Document: "what are the numbers, what is the hard evidence of things not working"
- Implement changes based on evidence
- **"Stick to the process of doing things like that long enough and you will become a profitable trader"**
