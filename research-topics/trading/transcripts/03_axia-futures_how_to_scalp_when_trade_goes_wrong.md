# How To Scalp When Trade Goes Wrong

**Source:** https://axiafutures.com/blog/how-to-scalp-when-trade-goes-wrong/
**Published:** 09/12/2022 | Views: 3.5k
**Instruments:** E-mini S&P 500 futures
**Category:** Price Ladder Lessons

---

## Core Principle

> "When you are scalping futures, you most likely will be using the order flow as your guiding light. This means, that you are adjusting to what you see."

> "You are much more flexible yet responsible for managing your risk well. Many times, you won't have a fixed stop. You will have a stop range in which you operate and you will adjust based on the incoming data."

---

## The Trade Setup

- **Market condition:** Trending day (uptrend)
- **Pattern:** Flag forming at the top extreme of the trend
- **Focus:** Order-flow observation
- **Objective:** Nice quick scalp (not high tick targets)

### Specific Price Ladder Reference Points

Starting at **9:09** on the chart:
- **Absorption level:** Around **70**
- **Optimal long entry:** **67** (in direction of the trending day)
- **Flag short fade entry:** **71**

---

## What Actually Happened

- **Entry:** Executed at price level **67**
- **Immediate adverse move:** 2-point drop to **65**
- Market declined faster than anticipated based on the structure

---

## The Decision Tree (Two Options)

### Option A: Exit if 67 Fails

- Watch the **65-67** zone
- If price attempts to reclaim **67** multiple times and fails each time → cover the long
- Logic: repeated failure at a level = trend context has changed, flag breakout is failing

### Option B: Use 65 as New Reference

- Treat **65** as new support reference (low of the flag)
- If **65** holds, add to position at **67** when price returns
- Hold remainder with target at **71** and beyond (above absorption at 70)
- Potential fade short at **71** if order flow shows rejection there

**Critical distinction:** Adding at 67 after a hold at 65 is a **planned scale-in at a structural level**, NOT an emotional average-down.

---

## Stop Management Technique

**No fixed stops for scalping.** Instead:

- Operate within a **"stop range"** -- a zone rather than a single price
- Adjust the stop dynamically based on incoming order flow data
- The stop range is defined by structural levels (in this case, the 65-67 zone)
- If the structural level breaks on volume → exit
- If it holds → trade remains valid

---

## Hard Rules

1. **Do NOT average down.** There is a critical difference between planned scale-in at structure and emotional averaging.
2. **Multiple failed attempts at a level = exit.** If 67 can't be reclaimed on multiple tries, get out.
3. **Don't let a losing scalp drift into swing-trading mode.** Stay focused and constantly check if you are sticking to scalping rules.
4. **Pre-set order flow rules must accommodate new information quickly.** Your plan should have contingencies built in before the trade.
5. **Absolutely straightforward decision-making.** No "what if, should have, would have."
6. **If you can't make the rules as clear as possible, stop scalping and make some time for defining the rules.**

---

## The Goal

> "The goal of scalping is to stay flexible and open-minded, not rigid and closed-minded."
