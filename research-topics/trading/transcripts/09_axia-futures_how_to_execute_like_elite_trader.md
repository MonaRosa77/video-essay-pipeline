## The Adverse Selection Principle

At the **20:49** mark, Bogdan explains:

> "When you want it (want the trade), it is hard to get it or you can't get it; when you don't want it or get the trade too easily, you probably do not want it."

### Practical Self-Check
- When was the last time you wanted a fill but didn't get it? How did those trades eventually perform?
- How did easily-filled trades perform?

The principle drives aggression on genuine opportunities.

## The 4 Trading Techniques

### Technique 1: In With Size Quickly, Out With Small Clips

Using the adverse selection principle, be aggressive entering as early as possible with **maximum size**. This means **"lifting the offer or hitting the bid, not passively sitting to get hit."**

After entry, lay down small clips on bid or offer depending on trade direction. This maintains a core position while taking incremental profits.

### Technique 2: Never Hold Full Size Offside

Example from the price ladder diagram:
- **100 lots** placed in the market (yellow box)
- **50 lots** sit just above (purple box) to exit if market reverses quickly
- You never want to hold your full 100-lot position offside
- The 50-lot exit order provides immediate damage control

### Technique 3: Stay Until Exhaustion

Since small clips have been laid down, you maintain a core position for final exit when the **"price ladder personality shifts."**

The key: scaling out gradually lets you capture the final extension of the move before reversal.

### Technique 4: Front Run Denial ("TGTBT")

When a setup looks **"Too Good To Be True"** -- act ahead of the potential reversal rather than waiting for confirmation. Requires additional steps (separate article promised).

## Trading Example 1: British Pound -- Brexit Amendment Vote

**Date:** January 29th, 2019
**Video timestamp:** Starts at **7:56**

Adam traded the Brexit amendment vote on GBP.

**Mistake made:** Adam "laid down the bids too quickly and too narrow," resulting in most of his position exiting before final exhaustion. The clips were placed too tightly -- he was out of most of his position before the move completed.

**Lesson:** Space clips wider to maintain exposure through to exhaustion.

## Trading Example 2: Bund -- US/China Comment

**Video timestamp:** Starts at **16:55**
