### Structure
Two distinct phases:

2. Press conference

### Key Learning Question

> "When a lot of participants enter aggressively short on the back of the news, but there is no follow-through in that direction, how can this impact the velocity of the next move in the opposite direction?"

**Answer (implied):** Failed aggression creates opposite velocity. When aggressive shorts get no follow-through, the reversal accelerates as those shorts cover. The lack of follow-through IS the signal.

## Execution 2: Scalping Around a Wrong Trade (Italian News)

**Video:** "AXIA Elite Trader Scalps Around A Big Trade That Goes Wrong"

### Context
- **Position:** 250 lots long in Bund
- **Date:** 12 September, midday

### Market Events (Timeline)

**Initial comment:** "5-Star will seek Tria resignation if they do not get 10 billion Euros for universal income"

**Denial comment (shortly after):** "Senior 5-Star source tells Reuters there is tension over budget resources but no threats to remove Tria"

### The Strategy

The trader held 250 lots long while market moved against the position. He then **"artfully minimized losses by keeping a cool head and scalping around his core position to profit from the possible mean reversion trade."**

### Key Learning Question

> "What is more likely to happen? Continuation of the short after the denial of the news has been confirmed or a short-term rebound long from the original price? What is the underlying logic?"

**Answer (implied):** When news is denied, the move based on that news should reverse. Mean reversion from the denial is more probable than continuation. The traders who sold on the initial headline are now wrong-footed.

### Chart Note
"Denial of the initial news causes the market to immediately drop and rebound"

**Price ladder execution starts at:** 10:59 (with trade debrief by Adam following)

## Key Principles Demonstrated

1. **News denial = mean reversion opportunity.** When a headline is denied/reversed, expect reversion not continuation.
2. **Failed aggression = opposite velocity.** Aggressive shorts without follow-through create fast reversals.
3. **Scalp around a core position when wrong-sided.** Don't dump 250 lots at the worst price. Scalp tactically to offset drawdown while maintaining the thesis.
4. **Two-phase news trading.** Rate decision vs. press conference often diverge. Treat them as separate events.
5. **Visualization process.** Elite execution requires mental rehearsal at the level of "the greatest sportsmen on earth."
