# Booklist: Power
## What power is, how it's produced, financial market power structures, US social structure

---

## I. WHAT IS POWER? (The Nature of Power Itself)

1. **The Prince** -- Niccolo Machiavelli
   The original text on acquiring and wielding political power. Short, brutal, practical. Everything after this is commentary.

2. **The 48 Laws of Power** -- Robert Greene
   Distills 3,000 years of power history into tactical laws. Draws from Machiavelli, Sun Tzu, Clausewitz, Kissinger. Useful as a pattern library for recognizing power moves.

3. **Discipline and Punish** -- Michel Foucault
   How power operates not through force but through systems of surveillance, normalization, and institutional design. The concept of "docile bodies" -- how institutions produce compliant subjects without overt coercion.

4. **The Rape of the Mind** -- Joost Meerloo
   How menticide (psychological coercion) works at mass scale. Written by a psychologist who survived Nazi occupation. Covers propaganda, conditioning, and how populations are made to accept power structures.

5. **Influence: The Psychology of Persuasion** -- Robert Cialdini
   The 6 psychological principles (reciprocity, scarcity, authority, consistency, liking, consensus) that underpin how power is exercised at the individual level.

---

## II. WHO HOLDS POWER IN AMERICA? (Social Structure)

6. **The Power Elite** -- C. Wright Mills (1956)
   The foundational text. Argues that America is run by an interlocking triad of military, corporate, and political leaders. Still the starting point for any serious analysis.

7. **Who Rules America?** -- G. William Domhoff
   The empirical companion to Mills. Traces actual networks -- corporate boards, policy planning groups, campaign finance, social clubs -- to show how the upper class dominates policy.

8. **The Power Elite and the State: How Policy is Made in America** -- G. William Domhoff
   Goes deeper into how coalitions within the elite shaped specific policies (Social Security Act, post-WWII economic order).

9. **Plutocracy in America: How Increasing Inequality Destroys the Middle Class** -- Ronald P. Formisano
   Documents the "infrastructure of inequality" -- both visible and hidden mechanisms that obstruct the majority from exercising basic economic and political rights.

10. **Dark Money: The Hidden History of the Billionaires Behind the Rise of the Radical Right** -- Jane Mayer
    How a small network of ultra-wealthy families (Koch, Scaife, Olin, Bradley) built an ideological infrastructure -- think tanks, university programs, judicial pipelines -- to reshape American politics.

---

## III. HOW POWER IS PRODUCED IN FINANCIAL MARKETS

11. **The Fed: The Inside Story of How the World's Most Powerful Financial Institution Drives the Markets** -- Martin Mayer
    How the Federal Reserve reinvented itself as markets replaced banks as the primary source of investment capital. The mechanics of how monetary power actually works.

12. **The Power and Independence of the Federal Reserve** -- Peter Conti-Brown
    Challenges the idea that the Fed is a neutral technocracy. Shows how personalities, ideologies, and law shape Fed decisions -- and who benefits.

13. **13 Bankers: The Wall Street Takeover and the Next Financial Meltdown** -- Simon Johnson & James Kwak
    How Wall Street's ideology, wealth, and political access among Washington policymakers created the conditions for the 2008 crisis. The revolving door made explicit.

14. **Money, Inflation and Business Cycles: The Cantillon Effect and the Economy** -- Arkadiusz Sieron
    The first deep study of the Cantillon Effect -- how new money creation benefits those closest to the money spigot (banks, asset holders) before flowing to everyone else. The mechanism behind "how the rich get richer" through monetary policy.

15. **An Engine, Not a Camera: How Financial Models Shape Markets** -- Donald MacKenzie
    How financial theory doesn't just describe markets -- it actively constructs them. Black-Scholes didn't model options pricing; it created the conditions for modern options markets to exist. Power through intellectual infrastructure.

16. **Hedged Out: Inequality and Insecurity on Wall Street** -- Megan Tobias Neely
    How Wall Street reproduces its own class structure internally through hiring, culture, and gatekeeping.

---

## IV. THE INTERSECTION (Money, State, and Social Control)

17. **Stabilizing an Unstable Economy** -- Hyman Minsky
    The "financial instability hypothesis" -- that stability itself breeds instability because participants take on increasing risk during good times. Explains the boom-bust cycle as a feature, not a bug, of the power structure.

18. **Spiderweb Capitalism: How Global Elites Exploit Frontier Markets** -- Kimberly Kay Hoang
    Award-winning ethnography of how global elites use offshore networks to move wealth. The mechanics of how power crosses borders through shell companies, intermediaries, and legal gray zones.

19. **The Path to Power** (The Years of Lyndon Johnson, Vol. 1) -- Robert Caro
    Not finance per se, but the single best study of how power is actually acquired in America. Caro spent decades documenting Johnson's rise from nothing to absolute political mastery. The methods -- favors, intelligence, leverage, timing -- apply everywhere including markets.

20. **Manufacturing Consent** -- Noam Chomsky & Edward Herman
    The "propaganda model" of how media produces consent for existing power arrangements. Five filters: ownership, advertising, sourcing, flak, ideology. The intellectual framework for understanding why power structures are invisible to most people.

---

## Suggested Reading Order

### Start here (the foundations):
- *The Power Elite* (Mills) -- understand the structure
- *The Prince* (Machiavelli) -- understand the logic
- *Discipline and Punish* (Foucault) -- understand the mechanism

### Then the financial layer:
- *The Fed* (Mayer) or *Power and Independence of the Fed* (Conti-Brown)
- *13 Bankers* (Johnson & Kwak)
- *Money, Inflation and Business Cycles* (Sieron) -- for the Cantillon Effect

### Then go deep:
- *The Path to Power* (Caro) -- how one person actually did it
- *Dark Money* (Mayer) -- how a network does it
- *Spiderweb Capitalism* (Hoang) -- how it goes global
